DisasterMap Terms of Use

1. Introduction:

1.1. Platform Overview:

DisasterMap is a digital platform dedicated to emergency mapping. As an open-source tool, it relies on the active contribution of its user community to provide up-to-date and relevant information.

1.2. Objectives:

The primary objective of DisasterMap is to provide a fast and reliable solution for populations in disaster-prone areas to report natural disasters. Additionally, it facilitates the reporting of urgent needs resulting from these events, thus promoting a coordinated and effective response by authorities, organizations, and volunteers.

1.3. Community Engagement:

By using and contributing to DisasterMap, every user actively participates in creating a supportive community dedicated to mutual assistance and disaster risk prevention.

1.4. Acceptance of the Terms:

Use of DisasterMap implies full and unconditional acceptance of these terms of use. Each user is invited to familiarize themselves with all the terms defined here to ensure responsible and respectful use of the platform.

2. Access and Registration:

2.1. Access to the Platform:

DisasterMap is open to all users who wish to contribute or benefit from its services, as long as the rules of use are respected. No registration is required to access the basic information provided by the platform.

2.2. Registration:

For access to advanced features, registration is necessary. This registration allows, among other things, the verification of reported data and the opportunity to report potentially inaccurate or false information.

2.3. Commitments at Registration:

By registering, the user commits to providing truthful and up-to-date information. Creating multiple accounts or using false identities is strictly prohibited.

2.4. Account Security:

It is the responsibility of each user to maintain the confidentiality of their credentials and password. Any suspicious or unauthorized activity should be reported immediately to the DisasterMap team.

2.5. Privacy and Personal Data:

Respecting privacy is crucial to us. To learn more about how we process and protect your data, please consult our privacy policy.

3. Use of the Service:

3.1. Service Accessibility:

DisasterMap is designed to be accessible to all users. For a better understanding of how our service works, we recommend reviewing our explanatory video and use case diagram.

3.2. User Commitments:

Each user commits to using DisasterMap in accordance with the rules outlined in this charter. The main goal of our platform is to provide aid and support during disasters. Any act, information, or content aimed at deceiving, harming, or exploiting other users is strictly prohibited.

3.3. Appropriate Use:

It is imperative that users do not use the service for malicious, deceptive, or any other purpose than what it was designed for. The use of false information, creating multiple accounts, or any attempt to manipulate the service is prohibited.

4. Interactions with Other Users:

4.1. Code of Conduct:

While using the platform, all users are required to behave in a manner that respects the dignity, rights, and freedom of others. Users must refrain from any conduct that could be harmful, offensive, or detrimental to other members or the platform's reputation.

4.2. Prohibited Behaviors:

Harassing, threatening, or intimidating any other user of the platform. Defaming, insulting, or slandering any individual, whether a platform user or not. Sharing or promoting discriminatory content based on race, gender, religion, nationality, disability, sexual orientation, or age. Spam: Sending unsolicited messages, promoting third-party services, or distributing irrelevant content is strictly prohibited.

4.3. Reporting Abuses:

Users are encouraged to report any abusive behavior or violation of this charter to the platform's administration team. Administrators will promptly review each report and take appropriate actions.

5. User-Generated Content:

5.1. Selection of Descriptions:

Users are required to choose predefined descriptions when reporting a disaster or event. These descriptions have been put in place to ensure clarity, consistency, and accuracy of the information shared on the platform.

5.2. Accuracy and Truthfulness of Information:

Even though descriptions are predefined, users must ensure that the information they share, such as location or event date, is accurate and truthful. Any misuse, such as intentionally false or misleading reporting, could result in sanctions.

5.3. No Photos:

To respect privacy, copyright, and other legal and ethical concerns, users are not allowed to post photos on the platform.

5.4. Content Responsibility:

While the platform provides predefined descriptions, users remain responsible for the information they share. The platform neither endorses nor guarantees the accuracy of the information provided by users. The platform also reserves the right to verify, refuse, or remove any contributions that do not meet our standards and principles.

5.5. Reporting Inappropriate Content:

If a user believes that a description selected by another user is incorrect or inappropriate, they are encouraged to use the platform's reporting system. The platform will review reports and take appropriate actions, which may include correcting information or imposing sanctions on the offending user.

7.1. Warning:

A first violation of the platform's rules may result in a warning to the user. This warning will inform the user of their non-compliant behavior and remind them to follow the rules.
